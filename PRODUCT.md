# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Senior talent and people operations leaders at financial services firms: heads of talent acquisition, people ops directors, and CHROs at hedge funds, private equity firms, investment banks, and asset managers. They are analytically rigorous, have seen every HR vendor pitch, and are deeply skeptical of anything that sounds like recruitment software. When they land on this page, they are assessing whether Arbour understands their world, not whether the product looks nice.

Secondary: COOs and managing directors who own the infrastructure decision and have final sign-off.

## Product Purpose

Arbour builds people intelligence infrastructure for financial services: a structured, queryable, AI-ready people graph that combines a firm's institutional data with proprietary market signals. The product exists because the same transformation that already happened to financial data (structured, queryable, AI-ready) hasn't happened yet for people data. Arbour is the infrastructure layer that makes that possible.

The landing page's job is to make a senior FS professional feel understood in the first 10 seconds and to surface a path to a discovery conversation. Conversion is a call, not a signup.

## Brand Personality

Intelligent, considered, calm. McKinsey-meets-software: structured thinking, quiet authority. The brand speaks like a senior practitioner who has lived the problem from the inside, not a vendor pitching a solution from the outside. It never oversells. It earns trust through precision and restraint.

Three words: precise, operator, unhurried.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI startups: purple gradients, floating orbs, "powered by AI" SaaS cream aesthetic, hero animations that exist to signal innovation rather than demonstrate it.
- LinkedIn and talent SaaS: pastel gradients, recruiter-speak, HR software visual language, stock-photo diversity imagery, "streamline your hiring" copy.
- Crypto / Web3 dark UI: neon on near-black, glitch effects, hype-forward energy.

## Design Principles

1. **Infrastructure over decoration.** The design should feel load-bearing: built to last, precise, not decorative. Every ornament must earn its place by doing structural work.
2. **Restraint is the signal.** In a world of AI startups screaming for attention, stillness communicates confidence. What's removed matters as much as what's kept.
3. **Practitioners, not vendors.** Copy and visual language should feel like they come from someone who has sat on the buy side of these decisions, not a software company pitching to them.
4. **Show the system, don't explain it.** The product is a people intelligence system. The site should demonstrate structured thinking and precision rather than describing it in abstract terms.
5. **Density is honesty.** Financial services professionals process dense information daily. Treating them like consumers who need everything simplified is a tell. The site can handle depth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respect prefers-reduced-motion for the hero canvas animation and any other motion. Ensure body text meets 4.5:1 contrast on all background colors. IBM Plex Mono at small sizes (10-11px) must remain legible: test on retina and non-retina.
